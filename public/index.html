The Expedition

A coastline, end to end

Base yourselves in Binz and sweep south along the island's most beautiful resort coast — from the quiet pines of Prora to the wild stone-sand shores of Göhren. This guide stitches every beach, train, break and euro into one easy rhythm.

Travel light, travel free

Getting There

Frankfurt → Rügen connections

Two regional-only corridors that work with the Deutschland-Ticket (€0). Legs are marked for how smooth they are. These are representative timings — always confirm live times in the DB Navigator app on the day, as regional night service is sparse and changes often.

Smooth — short, reliable transfer Caution — fewer services / medium wait Weak link — long night wait, plan a backup

Corridor A · via Berlin

Recommended

Fewest transfers · most reliable final legs (RE5 + RE9)

  1. Frankfurt (Main) Hbf → Erfurt Hbf
    RE🕓 ~2h40 ride⏱ wait ~15 min
  2. Erfurt → Leipzig Hbf
    RE🕓 ~1h20 ride⏱ wait ~30 min
  3. Leipzig → Berlin Hbf
    RE🕓 ~1h45 ride⏱ overnight wait ~1–2h
  4. Berlin Hbf → Stralsund Hbf
    RE5🕓 ~3h00 ride⏱ wait ~20 min
  5. Stralsund → Bergen → Ostseebad Binz
    RE9🕓 ~1h00 ride🏁 arrive
🔁 4 changes🚆 ride ~9h⏳ waits ~2–3h≈ 10–11h total

Corridor B · via Hamburg

Backup

More transfers, but a good fallback if the Berlin line is disrupted

  1. Frankfurt (Main) Hbf → Kassel-Wilhelmshöhe
    RE🕓 ~2h30 ride⏱ wait ~20 min
  2. Kassel → Göttingen → Hannover Hbf
    RE🕓 ~2h00 ride⏱ 2 short hops
  3. Hannover → Hamburg Hbf
    RE / metronom🕓 ~1h20 ride⏱ wait ~25 min
  4. Hamburg → Stralsund Hbf
    RE🕓 ~3h00 ride⏱ few evening services
  5. Stralsund → Ostseebad Binz
    RE9🕓 ~1h00 ride🏁 arrive
🔁 4–5 changes🚆 ride ~10h⏳ waits ~2h≈ 11–12h total
💡 Faster paid option: An ICE Frankfurt → Berlin (~4h) + RE9 onward gets you to Binz in roughly 7.5–8h with one easy change — but ICE/IC are not covered by the Deutschland-Ticket, so it costs extra per person. Keep it in your back pocket if a regional leg falls through.

🌤️ Smart tips for June

  • 🌊 Baltic water is brisk in early June (~15–17 °C) — great for wading, bracing for swimming.
  • 🧴 Long beach days = strong reflection. Pack high-SPF and a light cover-up.
  • 👟 Göhren's Südstrand is stony — water shoes make the wild beaches comfortable.
  • 🌧️ Showers likely on Jun 5 — a packable rain jacket beats a bulky umbrella in the wind.
  • 💨 Sea breeze drops the "feels-like" temp fast — bring a warm layer for evenings.
  • 🚌 Check the last Bus 20 back to Binz before settling in for sunset.
